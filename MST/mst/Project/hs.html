Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the tallest brick minaret in the world, standing at 72.5 meters. Built in 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and later completed by his successors, it showcases Indo-Islamic architecture with intricate carvings and inscriptions. The monument symbolizes the beginning of Muslim rule in India and remains a major tourist attraction.

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, was once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire. It features stunning temple ruins, stone-carved monuments, and the famous Virupaksha Temple. Surrounded by boulder-strewn landscapes, Hampi is a paradise for history lovers and travelers.

Jagannath Temple

üõï Eastern India

Located in Puri, Odisha, the Jagannath Temple is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, dedicated to Lord Jagannath. The temple is known for its annual Rath Yatra, where massive chariots carry the deities through the city. Its unique rituals, massive kitchen, and centuries-old traditions make it a revered spiritual destination.
